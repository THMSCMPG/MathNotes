Basic Integration

Evaluate \(\displaystyle \int (3x^2 + 4x - 5) \, dx\).

Approach 1: Power Rule for Integration

Power Rule for Integration

$$\int x^n \, dx = \frac{x^{n+1}}{n+1} + C \quad (n \neq -1)$$
1
Apply to Each Term
$$\int 3x^2 \, dx = 3 \cdot \frac{x^3}{3} = x^3$$ $$\int 4x \, dx = 4 \cdot \frac{x^2}{2} = 2x^2$$ $$\int -5 \, dx = -5x$$
2
Combine and Add Constant
$$\int (3x^2 + 4x - 5) \, dx = \boxed{x^3 + 2x^2 - 5x + C}$$
Don't forget the constant of integration C!

Approach 2: Term by Term Integration

1
Separate the Integral
$$\int (3x^2 + 4x - 5) \, dx = \int 3x^2 \, dx + \int 4x \, dx - \int 5 \, dx$$
2
Integrate Each Part

Increase exponent by 1, divide by new exponent:

$$= x^3 + 2x^2 - 5x + C$$
2

U-Substitution

Evaluate \(\displaystyle \int 2x(x^2 + 1)^3 \, dx\).

Approach 1: U-Substitution

Substitution Rule

If \(u = g(x)\), then \(du = g'(x)dx\) and: $$\int f(g(x))g'(x) \, dx = \int f(u) \, du$$
1
Choose Substitution

Let \(u = x^2 + 1\)

$$du = 2x \, dx$$
2
Substitute and Integrate
$$\int 2x(x^2 + 1)^3 \, dx = \int u^3 \, du$$ $$= \frac{u^4}{4} + C$$
3
Back-Substitute
$$= \boxed{\frac{(x^2 + 1)^4}{4} + C}$$

Approach 2: Pattern Recognition

1
Recognize the Pattern

Notice that \(2x\) is the derivative of \(x^2 + 1\)

2
Apply Chain Rule in Reverse

This integral has the form \(\int f'(x)[f(x)]^n \, dx\)

$$= \frac{(x^2 + 1)^4}{4} + C$$
With practice, you can recognize substitution patterns without explicitly substituting!

Definite Integrals and Area

Find the area between \(f(x) = x^2\) and the x-axis from \(x = 0\) to \(x = 2\).

Approach 1: Fundamental Theorem of Calculus

Fundamental Theorem of Calculus

$$\int_a^b f(x) \, dx = F(b) - F(a)$$ where \(F'(x) = f(x)\)
1
Find the Antiderivative
$$\int x^2 \, dx = \frac{x^3}{3}$$
2
Evaluate at Bounds
$$\int_0^2 x^2 \, dx = \left[\frac{x^3}{3}\right]_0^2$$ $$= \frac{2^3}{3} - \frac{0^3}{3}$$ $$= \boxed{\frac{8}{3} \text{ square units}}$$

Approach 2: Riemann Sum Approach

1
Conceptual Understanding

The area is the limit of Riemann sums as partition width approaches zero.

2
Apply Definition
$$\text{Area} = \lim_{n \to \infty} \sum_{i=1}^n f(x_i^*) \Delta x = \int_0^2 x^2 \, dx = \frac{8}{3}$$
Summary: Integration is the reverse of differentiation and allows us to find areas, volumes, and accumulated quantities. Master basic integration rules and substitution for success in multivariable calculus.
