Section 3.1

Applications of First-Order Linear DEs
This section explores first-order linear differential equations of the form $$\frac{dy}{dx} + P(x)y = f(x)$$ and their applications to growth/decay problems, mixing problems, temperature problems, and other real-world scenarios.
1

Exponential Growth and Decay

A radioactive substance decays at a rate proportional to the amount present. If 100g reduces to 80g in 10 years, find the amount after 30 years.

Approach 1: Direct DE Setup

1
Set Up the Model

Rate proportional to amount

$$\frac{dA}{dt} = -kA$$
2
Solve the DE

This is separable

$$A(t) = A_0 e^{-kt}$$
3
Find k Using Data

Use A(10) = 80 when A₀ = 100

$$80 = 100e^{-10k} \implies k = -\frac{1}{10}\ln(0.8) \approx 0.0223$$
4
Calculate A(30)

Substitute t = 30

$$A(30) = 100e^{-30k} = 100(0.8)^3 = 51.2 \text{ grams}$$

Approach 2: Half-Life Approach

1
Find Half-Life

Time for half to decay

$$t_{1/2} = \frac{\ln 2}{k}$$
2
Calculate from Given Data

From 100g to 80g in 10 years

$$k = \frac{1}{10}\ln\left(\frac{100}{80}\right)$$
3
Use Decay Formula

Apply exponential formula

$$A(t) = A_0\left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^{t/t_{1/2}}$$

Mixing Problems

A tank contains 100 gallons of brine (salt water) with 50 lbs of salt. Pure water enters at 5 gal/min, and mixture leaves at 5 gal/min. Find the amount of salt after 20 minutes.

Approach 1: Rate In - Rate Out Method

1
Define Variables

Let A(t) = pounds of salt at time t

2
Set Up Rate Equation

Rate of change = Rate In - Rate Out

$$\frac{dA}{dt} = R_{in} - R_{out}$$
3
Calculate Rates

Rate in = 0 (pure water)\nRate out = (5 gal/min)·(A/100 lbs/gal)

$$\frac{dA}{dt} = 0 - \frac{5A}{100} = -\frac{A}{20}$$
4
Solve the DE

Separable equation

$$A(t) = 50e^{-t/20}$$
5
Find A(20)

Substitute t = 20

$$A(20) = 50e^{-1} \approx 18.39 \text{ lbs}$$

Approach 2: Concentration Method

1
Track Concentration

Concentration = Amount/Volume

$$C(t) = \frac{A(t)}{V(t)}$$
2
Note Constant Volume

Volume stays at 100 gallons

$$C(t) = \frac{A(t)}{100}$$
3
Set Up DE for C

Derive equation for concentration

$$\frac{dC}{dt} = -\frac{C}{20}$$
4
Solve and Convert

Find C(t), then A(t) = 100C(t)

Newton's Law of Cooling

A cup of coffee at 90°C is placed in a room at 20°C. After 5 minutes, the temperature is 70°C. When will it reach 40°C?

Approach 1: Standard Newton's Law

1
State Newton's Law

Rate proportional to temperature difference

$$\frac{dT}{dt} = -k(T - T_s)$$
2
Identify Parameters

T_s = 20°C (surrounding)\nT(0) = 90°C\nT(5) = 70°C

3
Solve the DE

This gives

$$T(t) = T_s + (T_0 - T_s)e^{-kt} = 20 + 70e^{-kt}$$
4
Find k

Use T(5) = 70

$$70 = 20 + 70e^{-5k} \implies k = -\frac{1}{5}\ln\left(\frac{50}{70}\right)$$
5
Find Time for T = 40

Solve for t

$$40 = 20 + 70e^{-kt} \implies t \approx 13.86 \text{ minutes}$$
