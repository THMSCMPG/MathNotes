Matrix Multiplication

Compute the product \(AB\) where \(A = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ 3 & -1 \end{pmatrix}\) and \(B = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 4 \\ 2 & -2 \end{pmatrix}\).

Approach 1: Row-Column Method

Matrix Multiplication

The \((i,j)\) entry of \(AB\) is the dot product of row \(i\) of \(A\) with column \(j\) of \(B\).
1
Entry (1,1)

Row 1 of \(A\) ¬∑ Column 1 of \(B\):

$$(2)(1) + (1)(2) = 2 + 2 = 4$$
2
Entry (1,2)

Row 1 of \(A\) ¬∑ Column 2 of \(B\):

$$(2)(4) + (1)(-2) = 8 - 2 = 6$$
3
Entry (2,1)

Row 2 of \(A\) ¬∑ Column 1 of \(B\):

$$(3)(1) + (-1)(2) = 3 - 2 = 1$$
4
Entry (2,2)

Row 2 of \(A\) ¬∑ Column 2 of \(B\):

$$(3)(4) + (-1)(-2) = 12 + 2 = 14$$
5
Final Result
$$AB = \boxed{\begin{pmatrix} 4 & 6 \\ 1 & 14 \end{pmatrix}}$$

Approach 2: Systematic Computation

1
Set Up the Product
$$AB = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ 3 & -1 \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 4 \\ 2 & -2 \end{pmatrix}$$
2
Compute All Entries
$$= \begin{pmatrix} 2(1)+1(2) & 2(4)+1(-2) \\ 3(1)+(-1)(2) & 3(4)+(-1)(-2) \end{pmatrix}$$ $$= \begin{pmatrix} 4 & 6 \\ 1 & 14 \end{pmatrix}$$
Matrix multiplication is NOT commutative: \(AB \neq BA\) in general!

Solving Linear Systems

Solve the system: \(\begin{cases} 2x + y = 5 \\ x - y = 1 \end{cases}\)

Approach 1: Gaussian Elimination

1
Write Augmented Matrix
$$\left[\begin{array}{cc|c} 2 & 1 & 5 \\ 1 & -1 & 1 \end{array}\right]$$
2
Row Reduction

\(R_2 \leftarrow R_2 - \frac{1}{2}R_1\):

$$\left[\begin{array}{cc|c} 2 & 1 & 5 \\ 0 & -\frac{3}{2} & -\frac{3}{2} \end{array}\right]$$
3
Back Substitution

From row 2: \(-\frac{3}{2}y = -\frac{3}{2} \Rightarrow y = 1\)

Substitute into row 1: \(2x + 1 = 5 \Rightarrow x = 2\)

$$\boxed{x = 2, \quad y = 1}$$

Approach 2: Substitution Method

1
Solve for x from Second Equation
$$x - y = 1 \Rightarrow x = y + 1$$
2
Substitute into First Equation
$$2(y + 1) + y = 5$$ $$2y + 2 + y = 5$$ $$3y = 3 \Rightarrow y = 1$$
3
Find x
$$x = y + 1 = 1 + 1 = 2$$

Approach 3: Matrix Form

1
Express as Matrix Equation
$$\begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ 1 & -1 \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} x \\ y \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 5 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}$$
2
Find Inverse

For \(A = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ 1 & -1 \end{pmatrix}\), \(\det(A) = -2 - 1 = -3\)

$$A^{-1} = \frac{1}{-3}\begin{pmatrix} -1 & -1 \\ -1 & 2 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} \frac{1}{3} & \frac{1}{3} \\ \frac{1}{3} & -\frac{2}{3} \end{pmatrix}$$
3
Multiply by Inverse
$$\begin{pmatrix} x \\ y \end{pmatrix} = A^{-1}\begin{pmatrix} 5 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 2 \\ 1 \end{pmatrix}$$

Determinants

Find the determinant of \(C = \begin{pmatrix} 3 & 2 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 & 4 \\ 5 & 2 & 0 \end{pmatrix}\).

Approach 1: Cofactor Expansion (Row 2)

Cofactor Expansion

$$\det(A) = \sum_{j=1}^n (-1)^{i+j} a_{ij} M_{ij}$$ where \(M_{ij}\) is the \((i,j)\) minor.
1
Expand Along Row 2

Row 2 has a zero, making computation easier:

$$\det(C) = 0 \cdot M_{21} - 1 \cdot M_{22} + 4 \cdot M_{23}$$
2
Compute Minors
$$M_{22} = \begin{vmatrix} 3 & 1 \\ 5 & 0 \end{vmatrix} = 0 - 5 = -5$$ $$M_{23} = \begin{vmatrix} 3 & 2 \\ 5 & 2 \end{vmatrix} = 6 - 10 = -4$$
3
Calculate Determinant
$$\det(C) = 0 - 1(-5) + 4(-4)$$ $$= 0 + 5 - 16 = \boxed{-11}$$
Always expand along the row or column with the most zeros!

Approach 2: Rule of Sarrus

1
Repeat First Two Columns
$$\begin{array}{ccccc} 3 & 2 & 1 & 3 & 2 \\ 0 & 1 & 4 & 0 & 1 \\ 5 & 2 & 0 & 5 & 2 \end{array}$$
2
Add Downward Diagonals
$$3(1)(0) + 2(4)(5) + 1(0)(2) = 0 + 40 + 0 = 40$$
3
Subtract Upward Diagonals
$$1(1)(5) + 3(4)(2) + 2(0)(0) = 5 + 24 + 0 = 29$$
4
Final Result
$$\det(C) = 40 - 29 = 11$$
