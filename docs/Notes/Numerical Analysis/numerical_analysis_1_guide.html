Bisection Method

Use bisection to find a root of \(f(x) = x^3 - x - 2\) on \([1, 2]\) (one iteration).

Approach 1: Bisection Method

Bisection Principle

If \(f(a)\) and \(f(b)\) have opposite signs, a root exists in \([a,b]\). Test the midpoint.
1
Verify Conditions
$$f(1) = 1 - 1 - 2 = -2 < 0$$ $$f(2) = 8 - 2 - 2 = 4 > 0$$

Sign change confirms a root in [1,2]

2
Find Midpoint
$$c = \frac{a + b}{2} = \frac{1 + 2}{2} = 1.5$$
3
Evaluate at Midpoint
$$f(1.5) = (1.5)^3 - 1.5 - 2 = 3.375 - 1.5 - 2 = -0.125 < 0$$

Root is in \([1.5, 2]\) for next iteration.

2

Newton's Method

Perform one iteration of Newton's method on \(f(x) = x^2 - 2\) starting at \(x_0 = 1\).

Approach 1: Newton's Method

Newton's Formula

$$x_{n+1} = x_n - \frac{f(x_n)}{f'(x_n)}$$
1
Find Derivative
$$f'(x) = 2x$$
2
Apply Formula
$$x_1 = x_0 - \frac{f(x_0)}{f'(x_0)} = 1 - \frac{1^2 - 2}{2(1)}$$ $$= 1 - \frac{-1}{2} = 1 + 0.5 = \boxed{1.5}$$
Newton's method converges quadratically when close to the root!
